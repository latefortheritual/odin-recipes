Pasta Bolognese

Description

Bolognese isn't your typical tomato-based spaghetti sauce. It originated in Bologna, Italy, and is a hearty ragù that starts with a soffritto — that all-important blend of finely diced onion, carrot, and celery — loaded up with a variety of ground meats and cooked with white wine. Then, a relatively small amount of tomatoes are added along with stock; the sauce is cooked slow and low to develop and concentrate the flavors. To finish, it's enriched with a touch of cream.

Ingredients

  • tomatoes
  • carrot
  • meat

Steps

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Heat 1 tablespoon of the olive oil in a large, heavy saucepan until shimmering. Add the onion, carrot, celery, and pancetta and cook over moderate heat, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are softened but not browned, about 8 minutes. Scrape the vegetable mixture into a large bowl.
  3. Add the remaining 3 tablespoons of olive oil to the saucepan and heat until just shimmering. Add the beef, veal, and pork and cook over moderately high heat until just barely pink, about 5 minutes.
